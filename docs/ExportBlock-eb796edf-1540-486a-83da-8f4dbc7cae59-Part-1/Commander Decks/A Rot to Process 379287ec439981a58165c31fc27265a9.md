## Strategy & Playstyle

Dimir zombie aristocrats. Wilhelt generates endless Decayed tokens when zombies die, fueling sacrifice loops with Poppet Factory and Gravecrawler to drain the table through Plague Belcher, Vengeful Dead, and Mirkwood Bats.

**Primary Game Plan:** Build a board of Zombie tokens through Wilhelt's death trigger and tribal synergies, then drain opponents out via aristocrats payoffs like Plague Belcher, Vengeful Dead, and Mirkwood Bats whenever Zombies die.

**Secondary Lines:** If drain effects are removed, pivot to overwhelming combat with exponential token generation from Necroduality and Tombstone Stairwell, or use Rooftop Storm to cast the entire hand for free.

**Win Conditions:**

- Wilhelt + Poppet Factory + any free sac outlet (Carrion Feeder, Ashnod's Altar) → infinite death triggers → drain the table with Plague Belcher / Vengeful Dead / Mirkwood Bats
- Tombstone Stairwell generating massive death triggers each turn cycle with aristocrats payoffs on board
- Gray Merchant of Asphodel recurring via Gisa and Geralf / Lord of the Undead for repeated life drain

**Typical Game Arc:** Turns 1–3 deploy ramp and early Zombies. Turns 4–5 land Wilhelt and begin the sacrifice-token loop. Turns 6–8 assemble an aristocrats payoff + free sac outlet to threaten a kill, or establish Rooftop Storm for an explosive turn.

## Key Synergy Lines

### Poppet Factory Infinite Loop

**Cards:** Wilhelt, the Rotcleaver + Poppet Stitcher // Poppet Factory + Carrion Feeder (or Ashnod's Altar)

**Steps:**

1. With Poppet Factory on the battlefield (flipped side of Poppet Stitcher), sacrifice any Zombie to Carrion Feeder
2. Wilhelt triggers — create a decayed Zombie token, but Poppet Factory removes all abilities, so no decayed
3. Sacrifice the new token to Carrion Feeder, triggering Wilhelt again
4. Repeat indefinitely

**Result:** Infinite death triggers, infinite ETB triggers, infinite +1/+1 counters on Carrion Feeder (or infinite colorless mana with Ashnod's Altar). With any aristocrats payoff on board (Plague Belcher, Vengeful Dead, Mirkwood Bats, Diregraf Captain), this kills the table.

### Rooftop Storm + Gravecrawler Engine

**Cards:** Rooftop Storm + Gravecrawler + any sac outlet (Carrion Feeder / Ashnod's Altar / Warren Soultrader)

**Steps:**

1. Cast Gravecrawler for free via Rooftop Storm (it's a Zombie)
2. Sacrifice Gravecrawler to a sac outlet
3. Recast Gravecrawler from graveyard for free (you control a Zombie — Wilhelt counts)
4. Repeat

**Result:** Infinite death/sacrifice triggers. Drains the table with any aristocrats payoff. Generates infinite mana with Ashnod's Altar or infinite treasure with Warren Soultrader.

### Tombstone Stairwell Value Engine

**Cards:** Tombstone Stairwell + aristocrats payoffs (Plague Belcher, Diregraf Captain, Vengeful Dead)

**Steps:**

1. On each player's upkeep, Tombstone Stairwell creates Zombie tokens equal to creatures in that player's graveyard
2. At end of each turn, all Tombstone tokens die
3. Each death triggers your aristocrats payoffs, draining all opponents

**Result:** Massive drain each turn cycle — scales with graveyard size. In a 4-player game with stocked graveyards, this can deal 20+ damage per turn rotation without any other action.

## Strengths & Weaknesses

### Strengths

- **Built-in card advantage:** Wilhelt draws a card whenever a Zombie dies with no decayed limitation, and replaces dying Zombies with tokens — the deck rarely runs out of resources
- **Resilience through recursion:** Lord of the Undead, Gisa and Geralf, Havengul Lich, and Relentless Dead provide multiple angles to rebuild after board wipes
- **Compact win conditions:** The Poppet Factory combo requires only 3 cards (one is always in the command zone), and Gravecrawler loops are similarly compact

### Weaknesses

- **Graveyard hate is devastating:** Rest in Peace, Leyline of the Void, and Dauthi Voidwalker shut down Gravecrawler loops, Gisa and Geralf, and reduce Tombstone Stairwell's output significantly
- **Enchantment-based hate is hard to remove:** Dimir lacks enchantment removal entirely — cards like Rest in Peace or Overwhelming Splendor require Feed the Swarm or bouncing via Cyclonic Rift
- **Board-wipe vulnerable early:** Before assembling enough aristocrats payoffs, a well-timed Wrath of God can set the deck back significantly since the mana curve is relatively high for the engine pieces

## Matchup Notes

### Threats That Shut Us Down

- **Rest in Peace / Leyline of the Void:** Prevents all graveyard recursion and shuts down Gravecrawler loops entirely → Answer with Feed the Swarm or Cyclonic Rift; prioritize counters (Counterspell, Swan Song) when these are likely
- **Elesh Norn, Grand Cenobite:** Kills all Zombie tokens on creation (they enter as 2/2 decayed, become 0/0) → Remove immediately with Go for the Throat or Fatal Push; Toxic Deluge also handles this
- **Torpor Orb / Hushbringer:** Shuts down ETB-based aristocrats and Wilhelt's death replacement → Bounce with Cyclonic Rift or destroy with removal; these are less common but devastating

### Favorable Matchups

- **Creature-based aggro decks:** We outvalue them in the mid-game with free tokens and chip damage from aristocrats
- **Decks that ignore the graveyard:** Without hate, our recursion engines and Gravecrawler loops go unanswered

### Table Politics

- **Fly under the radar early:** Wilhelt looks threatening but doesn't win until combo pieces align — don't overextend into obvious board states that attract wraths
- **Threaten retribution:** With aristocrats payoffs on board, attacking into us is punished — leverage this politically

## Upgrade Strategy

The deck has a compact combo shell (Poppet Factory + Wilhelt + free sac outlet) but is one card short of a second infinite line: Phyrexian Altar with Gravecrawler creates colored mana loops that don't require Poppet Factory at all. Adding this redundancy means the deck doesn't fold to a single enchantment removal spell. The other upgrade axis is improving card draw density — currently at 6 sources, the deck sometimes stalls before finding a combo piece. Skullclamp would be transformative (equip to decayed tokens for draw-2) and is already owned in the collection. The budget path prioritises free sac outlets (Viscera Seer) and cheap lords that add damage to existing tokens. Prices from CardKingdom (via Archidekt/Scryfall ×1.3 estimate for unowned cards).

## Mulligan Guide *(optional)*

**Keep hands that have:**

- At least 3 lands (including a black source), 1 piece of ramp, and either a token producer or Wilhelt castable by turn 4
- A hand with 2 lands + 2 mana rocks + an early Zombie is keepable — the deck functions on 4 mana with Wilhelt out
- Any hand containing Gravecrawler + a sac outlet + an aristocrats payoff is a snap-keep regardless of land count above 2

**Mulligan aggressively if:**

- No black mana source, all high-CMC spells with no ramp, or a hand that does nothing until turn 5+
