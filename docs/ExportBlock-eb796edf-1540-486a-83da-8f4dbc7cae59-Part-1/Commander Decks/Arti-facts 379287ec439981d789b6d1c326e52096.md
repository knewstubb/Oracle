## Strategy & Playstyle

Azorius artifact aggro/combo. Deploys cheap artifacts and evasive creatures to trigger Mendicant Core's card draw on combat damage, then finishes with Cyberdrive Awakener turning the entire artifact board into flying attackers.

**Primary Game Plan:** Deploy cheap artifacts and evasive creatures early, equip Mendicant Core, Guidelight to connect for combat damage, then leverage the commander's triggered ability to generate card advantage and artifact synergies that snowball into a winning position.

**Secondary Lines:** If the commander is removed repeatedly, pivot to Kappa Cannoneer / Nettlecyst as standalone threats that grow with artifact density, or use Cyberdrive Awakener to turn all artifacts into flying attackers for a lethal alpha strike.

**Win Conditions:**

- Cyberdrive Awakener animating all artifacts for a massive flying alpha strike
- Kappa Cannoneer growing to lethal size with ward protection and evasion equipment
- Nettlecyst on an evasive creature — scales with artifact count
- Myr Battlesphere + Thousand Moons Smithy generating token boards

**Typical Game Arc:** Turns 1–2 deploy cheap artifacts and evasive 1-drops. Turn 3 land the commander and start connecting. Turns 4–6 snowball card advantage into finishers or Cyberdrive Awakener kill.

## Key Synergy Lines

### Scrap Trawler Value Chain

**Cards:** Scrap Trawler + cost-reducers (Etherium Sculptor / Foundry Inspector) + cheap artifacts

**Steps:**

1. With Scrap Trawler and a cost-reducer on board, cast cheap artifacts at reduced cost
2. When cheaper artifacts die (to Scour for Scrap or sacrifice), Scrap Trawler returns cheaper ones
3. Chain retrieval up the mana curve to keep artifacts flowing

**Result:** Recurring card advantage engine that rebuilds after removal.

### Cyberdrive Awakener Alpha Strike

**Cards:** Cyberdrive Awakener + artifact-dense board (8+ artifacts)

**Steps:**

1. Develop a board of mana rocks, equipment, and token artifacts over multiple turns
2. Cast Cyberdrive Awakener — all artifacts become 4/4 flying creatures until end of turn
3. Swing for 30+ flying damage in a single combat

**Result:** One-shot kill from a seemingly innocuous artifact board.

### Guidelight Pathmaker Tutor Line

**Cards:** Guidelight Pathmaker + key equipment/finisher targets

**Steps:**

1. Guidelight Pathmaker tutors artifact creatures or key equipment to hand
2. Deploy evasion equipment (Trailblazer's Boots, Whispersilk Cloak) on commander
3. Commander connects → triggers pile up value

**Result:** Consistent access to the right tool for each situation.

## Strengths & Weaknesses

### Strengths

- **Explosive card draw:** Mendicant Core drawing cards on combat damage plus Idol of Oblivion, Thopter Spy Network keep the hand full
- **Resilient to creature removal:** Many threats are non-creature artifacts that dodge creature wraths — Nettlecyst, equipment, and token generators survive Wrath of God
- **Fast clock with evasion:** Network Disruptor, Slither Blade, and equipment like Whispersilk Cloak ensure damage connects consistently

### Weaknesses

- **Artifact hate is devastating:** Vandalblast, Bane of Progress, and Collector Ouphe destroy the entire board state — the deck has minimal ways to recover from a resolved overloaded Vandalblast
- **Relies on combat damage:** Without evasion or a clear board, the commander's ability never triggers — pillowfort effects (Propaganda, Ghostly Prison) significantly slow the deck
- **Low interaction count:** Only 8 interaction pieces means the deck struggles to answer resolved threats — relies on racing rather than controlling

## Matchup Notes

### Threats That Shut Us Down

- **Vandalblast (overloaded) / Bane of Progress:** Destroys the entire game plan in one spell → Hold Swan Song / Spell Pierce specifically for these
- **Stony Silence / Null Rod:** Disables all artifact activated abilities (mana rocks, equipment) → Path to Exile or Swords to Plowshares the creature-based hate
- **Propaganda / Ghostly Prison:** Tax on attacks prevents the commander from connecting → Deploy evasion creatures that can attack profitably even with tax

### Favorable Matchups

- **Creature-based midrange:** Our evasive threats connect easily and we can out-draw them
- **Slow combo decks:** Fast clock pressure forces them to find answers before assembling

### Table Politics

- **Appear non-threatening early:** Small evasive creatures look harmless until the board suddenly becomes lethal with Cyberdrive Awakener
- **Offer information:** Commander connecting draws cards — position as "just drawing cards" rather than "building toward a kill"

## Upgrade Strategy

The deck's current build is solid at the evasion-aggro plan but lacks resilience — a single Vandalblast ends the game. Two upgrade paths are worth pursuing: (1) adding more artifact recursion (Emry, Dance of the Manse) so the deck can rebuild after sweepers, or (2) leaning harder into the Cyberdrive Awakener kill by adding more artifact token generators (Sai, Master Thopterist) that create a critical mass faster. The budget path focuses on cheap cards already in the collection that improve consistency (Thought Monitor for draw, Mystic Forge for card advantage), while the premium path adds Urza's Saga as a land-slot tutor and Enlightened Tutor for combo speed. Note that several key cards (Kappa Cannoneer, Mystic Forge, Cyberdrive Awakener, Simulacrum Synthesizer) are shared with mURZAnary tactics — adding second copies would free up allocation conflicts. ## Mulligan Guide *(optional)*

**Keep hands that have:**

- 2–3 lands, at least one cheap artifact or evasive creature, and a way to cast the commander by turn 3
- A hand with a cost-reducer (Etherium Sculptor) + multiple cheap artifacts is ideal — curves into commander triggers immediately
- Any hand with early evasion + draw engine (Thopter Spy Network, Bident of Thassa) is keepable with 3 lands

**Mulligan aggressively if:**

- No artifacts in hand, no creatures to connect with, or all expensive spells with fewer than 3 lands
