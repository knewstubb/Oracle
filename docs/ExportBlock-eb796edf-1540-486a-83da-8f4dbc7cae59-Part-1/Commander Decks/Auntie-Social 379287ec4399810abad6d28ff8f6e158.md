## Strategy & Playstyle

Jund -1/-1 counter engine (precon mod). Auntie Ool converts counter placement into card draw and life drain. Builds an asymmetric lock with wither creatures and Midnight Banshee while stealing opponents' creatures via Necroskitter.

**Primary Game Plan:** Use Auntie Ool's triggered ability to convert -1/-1 counter placement into a card advantage engine. Every counter on your own creatures draws a card, every counter on opponents' creatures drains life. Build an overwhelming board by out-drawing the table while weakening opposing creatures.

**Secondary Lines:** Theft and attrition — Necroskitter and The Reaper, King No More steal creatures that die with -1/-1 counters, converting opponent resources into your own. If the draw engine is disrupted, pivot to wither-based board control with Kulrath Knight and Midnight Banshee locking down combat.

**Win Conditions:**

- Blowfly Infestation + Flourishing Defenses loop (generates tokens from each death-with-counter, triggering further counter placement and draws)
- Devoted Druid + Auntie Ool (repeatable mana + card draw until Druid dies — generates massive card advantage and mana in a single turn)
- Midnight Banshee + Everlasting Torment (all nonblack creatures shrink every upkeep, eventually clearing the board while draining with Auntie Ool)
- Obelisk Spider draining each opponent whenever any -1/-1 counter lands, winning through incremental life loss

**Typical Game Arc:** Turns 1–3 ramp and deploy Auntie Ool. Turns 4–6 establish counter-placing engines (Channeler Initiate, Dusk Urchins, Contagion Clasp) to draw cards aggressively. Turns 7+ use accumulated card advantage and board control to lock opponents out with Kulrath Knight/Midnight Banshee or win via Blowfly loops and drain.

## Key Synergy Lines

### Devoted Druid Draw Engine

**Cards:** Devoted Druid + Auntie Ool, Cursewretch

**Steps:**

1. Tap Devoted Druid for {G}
2. Untap Devoted Druid by putting a -1/-1 counter on it
3. Auntie Ool triggers — draw a card
4. Repeat until Druid has 2 counters (dies as a 0/1)

**Result:** 2 mana and 2 cards drawn from a single 2-drop creature. Repeatable each time Druid enters.

### Blowfly Infestation Chain

**Cards:** Blowfly Infestation + Flourishing Defenses + any creature dying with a -1/-1 counter

**Steps:**

1. A creature with a -1/-1 counter dies
2. Blowfly Infestation triggers — put a -1/-1 counter on another creature
3. Flourishing Defenses triggers from the new counter — create a 1/1 Elf token
4. Auntie Ool triggers — draw a card (if your creature) or drain 1 (if opponent's)
5. If the new target dies from the counter, repeat from step 2

**Result:** Cascading token generation, card draw, and potential board wipe against small creatures. Can loop indefinitely with enough targets.

### Cauldron of Souls Recursion

**Cards:** Cauldron of Souls + Auntie Ool + any creatures

**Steps:**

1. Activate Cauldron of Souls giving your creatures persist
2. Your creatures die (from combat, sacrifice, or board wipe)
3. They return with a -1/-1 counter (persist)
4. Each returning creature triggers Auntie Ool — draw a card per creature

**Result:** Board wipe protection that doubles as card draw. Synergises with Hapatra (makes snakes from the persist counters).

### Wither Lock

**Cards:** Kulrath Knight + Midnight Banshee + Everlasting Torment

**Steps:**

1. Everlasting Torment makes all damage dealt wither (places -1/-1 counters instead)
2. Midnight Banshee puts a -1/-1 counter on each nonblack creature every upkeep
3. Kulrath Knight prevents creatures with -1/-1 counters from attacking or blocking
4. Auntie Ool drains opponents for each counter placed on their creatures

**Result:** Opponents' nonblack creatures are permanently locked out of combat and slowly die. You drain life each upkeep and draw from your own black creatures receiving counters.

## Strengths & Weaknesses

### Strengths

- Massive card advantage density — Auntie Ool triggers on every counter placement, often drawing 3–5 cards per turn cycle once engines are online
- Wither creatures serve double duty as removal and draw triggers — Skinrender, Incremental Blight, and Black Sun's Zenith kill things while fuelling the engine
- Theft subtheme (Necroskitter, The Reaper, Puppeteer Clique) punishes opponents for letting their creatures die with counters, creating card advantage asymmetry
- Resilient to creature-based aggro — wither and counter-based removal naturally shrinks opposing boards without needing traditional sweepers

### Weaknesses

- Graveyard hate (Rest in Peace, Bojuka Bog) shuts off Puppeteer Clique, Persist, and Aberrant Return — removing key recursion angles
- Indestructible creatures (Darksteel Colossus, Avacyn) ignore the entire -1/-1 counter removal package since counters can't kill them
- Enchantment/artifact removal hitting Blowfly Infestation or Flourishing Defenses cripples the token/draw engine — the deck runs limited enchantment protection
- Commander removal is devastating — Auntie Ool is the draw engine; without her, counter placement generates no card advantage, just removal
- Exile-based removal (Swords to Plowshares, Path to Exile) prevents death triggers and Necroskitter theft

## Matchup Notes

### Threats That Shut Us Down

- **Torpor Orb / Hushbringer:** Prevents ETB-based counter placement from Skinrender, Channeler Initiate → Sidestep by relying on non-ETB sources (Midnight Banshee, Contagion Clasp activation, Black Sun's Zenith)
- **Rest in Peace:** Exiles Puppeteer Clique targets, prevents Persist/Aberrant Return recursion → Answer with Wickerbough Elder, Assassin's Trophy, or Binding the Old Gods
- **Solitude / Swords to Plowshares on Auntie Ool:** Exiling the commander repeatedly is expensive to recover from → Hold Auntie Ool until you can protect her or have enough engine pieces to function without her
- **Indestructible board states (Avacyn, Eldrazi):** -1/-1 counters can't kill indestructible creatures, and Kulrath Knight lock doesn't matter if they have trample → Use Aether Snap to wipe all counters, or Vraska's -9

### Favorable Matchups

- Creature-based aggro (go-wide tokens, tribal) — wither and Midnight Banshee naturally shrink their board while you draw cards
- Voltron strategies — single large creatures are vulnerable to Skinrender, Incremental Blight, and accumulated counters; Kulrath Knight locks them out of combat
- Reanimator — Necroskitter steals their reanimation targets if they die with counters

### Table Politics

- Fly under the radar early — Auntie Ool looks harmless until the engine starts drawing 4+ cards per turn; avoid threatening the table until you have answers ready
- Let the drain happen passively — opponents lose 1 life per counter on their creatures, which adds up without looking aggressive; point out that you're "just drawing cards" rather than actively attacking

## Upgrade Strategy

This deck has two clear upgrade paths. Path 1: lean into the Blowfly Infestation loop by adding damage payoffs that trigger off -1/-1 counters (All Will Be One converts every counter placement into direct player damage, creating lethal drain alongside the existing token engine). Path 2: improve the deck's weakest area — card draw beyond Auntie Ool herself. Currently at only 4 dedicated draw spells, the deck folds to repeated commander removal. Adding Skullclamp (already owned) turns the 1/1 Elf/Insect tokens from Flourishing Defenses into draw-2 machines. The Sol Ring removal is still outstanding per pod rules, which opens a swap slot. The precon still has 5 remaining modification slots available, so there's room to add multiple pieces without exceeding limits. ## Mulligan Guide *(optional)*

**Keep hands that have:**

- 3+ lands including at least one black source and one green source (need both early for Auntie Ool on turn 3–4)
- At least one ramp spell or mana dork (Ignoble Hierarch, Devoted Druid, Nature's Lore) to accelerate Auntie Ool
- One counter-placing engine piece (Channeler Initiate, Dusk Urchins, Contagion Clasp) to start drawing immediately after commander lands

**Mulligan aggressively if:**

- Fewer than 3 lands, or no black/green sources — can't cast Auntie Ool on curve without both colors
- Hand is all expensive spells (5+ CMC) with no ramp — deck needs early counter engines online by turn 4–5 to function
