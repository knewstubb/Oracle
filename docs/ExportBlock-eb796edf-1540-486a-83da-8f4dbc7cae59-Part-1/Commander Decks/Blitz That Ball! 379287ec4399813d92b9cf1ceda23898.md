## Strategy & Playstyle

Bant +1/+1 counters and proliferate deck heavily modified from the Counter Blitz precon. This is an earlier, more powerful version of the Tidus counter strategy (now superseded by the precon-legal Counter Blitz build). Tidus moves counters between creatures and triggers draw + proliferate on combat damage. The deck layers multiple counter doublers (Hardened Scales, Tekuthal), premium draw engines (Danny Pink, Sphere Grid), and a high-value mana base to create a rapidly escalating board that overwhelms through combat.

**Primary Game Plan:** Deploy counter-generating creatures and multipliers (Hardened Scales, Tromell), cast Tidus turn 4, then attack with counter-bearing creatures. Each hit draws cards and proliferates — Tekuthal doubles every proliferate. Danny Pink draws a card on every counter placement. The compound growth rate means the board doubles in power every 2–3 turns.

**Secondary Lines:** Walking Ballista scales infinitely with proliferate and serves as both removal and finisher. Maester Seymour's monstrosity places counters equal to total board counters onto one creature for a lethal alpha strike. Damning Verdict clears all opponents' creatures while your counter-laden board survives.

**Win Conditions:**

- Damning Verdict + alpha strike (asymmetric wipe leaving your board, opponents left empty)
- Walking Ballista grown by proliferate (remove counters to machine-gun opponents directly)
- Maester Seymour monstrosity burst (30+ counters concentrated onto one attacker)
- Simic Ascendancy reaching 20 growth counters (alternate win condition from proliferate)

**Typical Game Arc:** Turns 1–3 deploy Hardened Scales, Tromell, ramp. Turn 4 Tidus arrives. Turns 5–7 compound counters with Tekuthal double-proliferate, Danny Pink drawing 3+ cards per turn. Turn 8+ board is 40+ total power with Damning Verdict or Walking Ballista closing.

## Key Synergy Lines

### Danny Pink Hyper-Draw

**Cards:** Danny Pink + Tromell + Hardened Scales + any creature entering

**Steps:**

1. Creature enters → Tromell places a +1/+1 counter on it
2. Hardened Scales adds an additional counter (now 2 counters)
3. Danny Pink triggers once per counter-placement event — draw a card
4. With multiple creatures entering per turn, draw 2–4 extra cards per turn cycle

**Result:** Every creature entering is a cantrip that also grows. Card advantage compounds with the board.

### Tekuthal Double Proliferate

**Cards:** Tekuthal, Inquiry Dominus + Tidus combat trigger + board full of counters

**Steps:**

1. Attack with counter-bearing creatures, dealing combat damage
2. Tidus triggers — draw a card, then proliferate
3. Tekuthal doubles the proliferate — proliferate happens twice instead of once
4. Every counter on every permanent grows by 2 per combat instead of 1

**Result:** Doubling the proliferate rate means the board's growth curve is exponential rather than linear. 10 total counters become 12, then 14, then 16 — each combat.

### Damning Verdict Board Lock

**Cards:** Damning Verdict + board of creatures all bearing +1/+1 counters

**Steps:**

1. Stack counters on all your creatures naturally through the deck's engine
2. Cast Damning Verdict (destroy all creatures without +1/+1 counters)
3. Your entire board survives (Tromell ensures everything has counters on ETB)
4. Opponents' boards are destroyed

**Result:** One-sided Wrath that requires zero additional setup — the counter strategy IS the setup.

### Walking Ballista Proliferate Kill

**Cards:** Walking Ballista + proliferate effects (Tidus, Tekuthal, Inexorable Tide)

**Steps:**

1. Deploy Walking Ballista with X counters
2. Each proliferate grows Ballista by 1–2 counters for free (no mana spent)
3. Remove counters to deal damage directly to creatures or players
4. Proliferate refills the counters; repeat indefinitely

**Result:** Machine gun that reloads itself every combat step. With Tekuthal doubling proliferate, Ballista grows faster than you can spend it.

## Strengths & Weaknesses

### Strengths

- **Deep counter density with multiple draw engines:** Danny Pink, Bred for the Hunt, Tidus, and Sphere Grid provide 3–5 draws per turn cycle — never runs out of gas
- **Asymmetric board wipe built in:** Damning Verdict is a one-sided Plague Wind in this deck, requiring no additional setup
- **Premium mana base:** Breeding Pool, Hallowed Fountain, Temple Garden, Bountiful Promenade ensure consistent turn 1–3 plays in all three colors
- **Smothering Tithe + Cyclonic Rift:** Two of the most powerful cards in Commander provide mana generation and a board reset respectively

### Weaknesses

- **Heavily modified beyond precon rules:** ~30+ card changes and ~$403 value means this is a custom deck, not a legal precon mod
- **Solemnity shuts everything down:** Prevents all counters — the deck literally cannot function. No enchantment removal in Bant beyond Bane of Progress
- **Vulnerable to mass exile:** Farewell, Cyclonic Rift (from opponents), and Merciless Eviction bypass counter-based protection entirely
- **Superseded by Counter Blitz:** This build is shelved in favor of the precon-legal version; some cards have been moved between builds

## Matchup Notes

### Threats That Shut Us Down

- **Solemnity:** Prevents all +1/+1 counters. Total shutdown. → Bane of Progress or pray for Cyclonic Rift to bounce it. Hold interaction for this card specifically.
- **Toxic Deluge:** -X/-X ignores indestructible and counter protection. → Protection Magic in response; accept the loss and rebuild with card draw.
- **Drannith Magistrate:** Prevents casting from exile (affects some interactions). → Path to Exile removes cleanly.

### Favorable Matchups

- Any deck without mass removal — the board compounds faster than spot removal can keep up
- Creature combat mirrors — our creatures grow while theirs stay static
- Graveyard decks — we don't use the graveyard; their hate is irrelevant

### Table Politics

- This deck is visibly powerful (Smothering Tithe, Cyclonic Rift) — expect to be targeted. Deploy protection early.
- The counter theme looks fair until Walking Ballista starts pinging and Damning Verdict lands. The perception gap buys 1–2 turns.

## Mulligan Guide *(optional)*

**Keep hands that have:**

- 3+ lands covering green + one other color, plus a 1–2 drop creature or Hardened Scales — the deck needs board presence before Tidus arrives turn 4
- Smothering Tithe or a counter-doubler (Hardened Scales, Tekuthal) with adequate mana — these cards define games when deployed early
- Danny Pink + any counter-generating creature — this combination provides immediate card advantage that snowballs

**Mulligan aggressively if:**

- Fewer than 3 lands with no ramp — the deck has a medium curve that requires consistent mana development
- No creatures under 4 CMC — without early board presence, Tidus arrives with nothing to attack alongside
