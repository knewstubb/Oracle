# Enchantress

Bracket: 2
Card Count: 89
Colours: B, G, W
Commander: Anikthea, Hand of Erebos
Deck URL: https://archidekt.com/decks/20391880
Last Updated: June 9, 2026

## Strategy & Playstyle

Abzan enchantress reanimator. Mills enchantments into the graveyard, then Anikthea brings them back as creature tokens. Token doublers (Doubling Season, Parallel Lives) multiply this output exponentially while enchantress draw engines keep the hand full.

**Primary Game Plan:** Mill enchantments into the graveyard via self-mill effects, then use Anikthea to reanimate them as creature tokens — effectively doubling the value of every enchantment. Token doublers (Doubling Season, Parallel Lives, Anointed Procession) multiply this output exponentially.

**Secondary Lines:** If Anikthea is removed repeatedly, lean on the enchantress draw engine (Argothian Enchantress, Sythis, Enchantress's Presence) to bury opponents in card advantage, using enchantment-based removal and token generation to control the board without the commander.

**Win Conditions:**

- Anikthea reanimating high-impact enchantments as tokens with doublers out — overwhelming board presence
- Concordant Crossroads granting haste to a sudden army of enchantment creature tokens
- Doomwake Giant constellation triggers wiping opponent boards while building yours
- Token doublers + any token-producing enchantment creating exponential board states

**Typical Game Arc:** Turns 1–3 deploy ramp and enchantress draw engines. Turns 4–5 begin self-milling to stock the graveyard with targets. Turn 5+ cast Anikthea and start reanimating enchantments as token creatures, snowballing with doublers.

## Key Synergy Lines

### Self-Mill into Anikthea Reanimation

**Cards:** Cemetery Tampering / Mesmeric Orb / Ripples of Undeath + Anikthea, Hand of Erebos

**Steps:**

1. Deploy self-mill enchantments early to stock the graveyard with enchantments
2. Cast Anikthea — she attacks to exile enchantments from graveyard and create token copies as creatures
3. Attack each turn to reanimate the highest-impact enchantment in the graveyard
4. With doublers, each attack creates multiple copies

**Result:** Repeatable reanimation that converts milled enchantments into board presence.

### Token Doubler Multiplication

**Cards:** Doubling Season / Parallel Lives / Anointed Procession + Anikthea attacks

**Steps:**

1. Deploy one or more token doublers before Anikthea starts attacking
2. Each Anikthea attack trigger creates a token copy → doubled by each active doubler
3. With two doublers active, each attack creates 4 tokens instead of 1

**Result:** Exponential token generation — a single combat step can create 4+ enchantment creature tokens.

### Enchantress Draw Engine

**Cards:** Sythis + Argothian Enchantress + Enchantress's Presence + cheap enchantments

**Steps:**

1. Deploy 2+ enchantress effects early
2. Every enchantment cast draws 2–3 cards
3. Chain enchantments together, drawing into more enchantments and ramp
4. Stock hand and graveyard simultaneously

**Result:** Massive card advantage — the deck can draw 5–10 cards per turn in the mid-game.

## Strengths & Weaknesses

### Strengths

- **Explosive card draw:** 5+ enchantress effects mean every enchantment cast replaces itself and more — the deck rarely runs out of gas
- **Resilient to creature removal:** Enchantments survive creature board wipes (Wrath of God only kills tokens, not the enchantments generating value)
- **Graveyard as a second hand:** Self-mill turns the graveyard into a toolbox Anikthea can access repeatedly

### Weaknesses

- **Enchantment-targeted hate is backbreaking:** Back to Nature, Cleansing Nova (enchantments mode), or Austere Command destroys the entire engine — and Abzan colors can't counter spells
- **Graveyard hate stops Anikthea's reanimation:** Rest in Peace or Leyline of the Void means Anikthea has no targets to exile → Sterling Grove and Heroic Intervention are the only protection
- **Commander-dependent for the primary win condition:** Without Anikthea, the deck draws cards but struggles to close — token doublers without Anikthea's reanimation are less impactful

## Matchup Notes

### Threats That Shut Us Down

- **Farewell:** Exiles artifacts, enchantments, creatures, AND graveyards in one spell → No clean answer; play Sterling Grove proactively
- **Rest in Peace / Leyline of the Void:** Empties the graveyard and prevents future stocking → Aura Shards can remove these if you have creatures entering
- **Drannith Magistrate:** Prevents casting commanders from command zone → Remove immediately with enchantment-based removal or Swords to Plowshares

### Favorable Matchups

- **Creature-based aggro:** Doomwake Giant constellation triggers wipe small creatures; token doublers outpace their board
- **Non-interactive combo:** If they don't interact, our card advantage buries them before they assemble

### Table Politics

- **Appear as "just drawing cards" early:** Enchantress engines look harmless — opponents often ignore you until the token explosion happens
- **Leverage removal enchantments politically:** Grasp of Fate and Touch the Spirit Realm can be pointed at the biggest threat to buy goodwill

## Mulligan Guide *(optional)*

**Keep hands that have:**

- 3+ lands (including at least one green source for ramp), 1 enchantress effect, and 1–2 cheap enchantments to start the draw chain
- A hand with Entomb/Buried Alive + ramp is keepable — stocks the graveyard for Anikthea to exploit
- Any hand with Sythis or Argothian Enchantress + 2 enchantments + 3 lands is excellent

**Mulligan aggressively if:**

- No green mana source, no enchantress effects AND no ramp, or a hand of all 5+ CMC cards with no acceleration
