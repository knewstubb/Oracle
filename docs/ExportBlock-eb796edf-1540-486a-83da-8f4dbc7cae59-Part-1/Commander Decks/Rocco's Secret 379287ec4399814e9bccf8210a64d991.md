## Strategy & Playstyle

Naya ETB-damage combo deck. Rocco tutors creatures directly to the battlefield, assembling a ping engine (Purphoros, Impact Tremors, Panharmonicon) that kills the table through non-combat damage whenever creatures enter.

**Primary Game Plan:** Use Rocco, Cabaretti Caterer as a repeatable creature tutor to assemble an ETB-damage engine. Deploy creatures that trigger Purphoros, God of the Forge / Impact Tremors / Witty Roastmaster to deal damage to all opponents whenever any creature enters the battlefield — then multiply triggers with Panharmonicon.

**Secondary Lines:** If the ETB-damage engines are removed, pivot to go-wide combat with Cathars' Crusade and Champion of Lambholt making creature tokens into massive, unblockable threats. Warstorm Surge provides single-target removal that also pressures life totals.

**Win Conditions:**

- Purphoros, God of the Forge + mass creature entry (Norin the Wary, Saltskitter, token generators) → 2 damage per creature per opponent per trigger
- Panharmonicon doubling ETB damage from Impact Tremors / Witty Roastmaster / General Kreat across multiple creatures entering
- Cathars' Crusade + token generators → exponentially growing army for lethal combat
- Warstorm Surge turning every creature into a removal spell AND face damage

**Typical Game Arc:** Turns 1–3 deploy mana dorks and ramp aggressively (14 ramp pieces). Turn 4–5 cast Rocco to tutor a key engine piece (Purphoros or Panharmonicon). Turns 5–7 snowball ETB triggers to kill the table, often winning through incidental damage without needing to attack.

## Key Synergy Lines

### Norin the Wary Ping Engine

**Cards:** Norin the Wary + Purphoros, God of the Forge (or Impact Tremors / Witty Roastmaster)

**Result:** 6–8 damage per turn rotation to each opponent with zero additional investment. With Panharmonicon, doubles to 4 damage per trigger.

### Rocco Tutor Chain

**Cards:** Rocco, Cabaretti Caterer (commander) + creature toolbox

**Result:** Repeatable tutor that builds the engine one piece at a time while also dealing damage on entry. Cast for X=4 to find Purphoros, X=2 for Norin/Saltskitter.

### Panharmonicon Multiplication

**Cards:** Panharmonicon + any ETB damage source (Impact Tremors, Purphoros, General Kreat)

**Result:** Each creature entering triggers damage sources TWICE. With Purphoros + Impact Tremors + Panharmonicon, each creature deals 6 damage to each opponent on entry.

### Saltskitter + Kodama Value

**Cards:** Saltskitter + Kodama of the East Tree + ETB damage engines

**Result:** Free, repeatable ETB triggers on opponents' turns with bonus free permanents via Kodama.

## Strengths & Weaknesses

### Strengths

- **Tutoring commander:** Rocco finds ANY creature up to X CMC directly to battlefield
- **Non-combat damage:** Wins through ETB triggers — blocking is irrelevant, Purphoros (indestructible) survives creature wraths
- **Redundant engines:** 15 "Engine" category cards means removing one still leaves multiple damage sources active

### Weaknesses

- **Torpor Orb effects are devastating:** Hushbringer, Tocatli Honor Guard, or Torpor Orb completely shut down the entire strategy
- **Enchantment/artifact removal hurts:** Panharmonicon, Impact Tremors are non-creature permanents vulnerable to Bane of Progress
- **Slow without ramp:** Needs 4+ mana to start operating; a stumbled opening means the engine comes online turns behind schedule

## Matchup Notes

### Threats That Shut Us Down

- **Torpor Orb / Hushbringer:** Prevents ALL ETB triggers → Remove with Generous Gift or Austere Command
- **Elesh Norn, Grand Cenobite:** Kills Norin, Saltskitter, and utility creatures → Swords to Plowshares or Blasphemous Act
- **Drannith Magistrate:** Prevents casting Rocco from command zone → Remove immediately

### Favorable Matchups

- Creature-based aggro without interaction (can't stop ping damage)
- Decks that cast many spells (more Norin triggers = more free damage)

### Table Politics

- Present as "group slug" early — damage hits everyone equally
- Sandbag Purphoros — let opponents use removal on other threats first

## Upgrade Strategy

The deck's primary weakness is fragility to ETB-hate (Torpor Orb, Hushbringer) and a lack of instant-speed tutors to find answers. Two upgrade paths worth pursuing:

**1. More tutors and protection** — Ensure the engine assembles reliably even through interaction. Chord of Calling (already owned) finds any creature at instant speed by convoking off your board. Eldritch Evolution ($4.49) sacs a 2-drop mana dork to put Purphoros directly onto the battlefield for 3 mana. Grand Abolisher ($22.99) locks opponents out of interacting on your combo turn. Birthing Pod ($17.99) provides repeatable tutoring up the CMC curve.

**2. Redundant/harder-to-remove damage sources** — Add threats that don't rely purely on ETB triggers. Terror of the Peaks ($39.99) deals damage equal to creature's power on entry — scales far better than Impact Tremors and survives as a 5/4 flyer. Goblin Bombardment ($4.99, currently in Big Zurgs) converts any creature into direct damage at instant speed — enables lethal math and dodges exile. Aura Shards ($22.99) proactively destroys the artifact/enchantment hate pieces that shut us down.

The budget-friendly start: Chord of Calling (free — already owned) + Eldritch Evolution ($4.49) + Flameshadow Conjuring ($4.99). These three cards dramatically improve consistency for under $10. ## Mulligan Guide

**Keep hands that have:**

- 3 lands (at least one green), 1–2 ramp pieces, and Rocco castable by turn 4 or an early engine piece
- Birds/Llanowar Elves + 2 lands + Purphoros/Panharmonicon is excellent

**Mulligan if:** No green source, all expensive cards with no acceleration, can't interact until turn 5+
