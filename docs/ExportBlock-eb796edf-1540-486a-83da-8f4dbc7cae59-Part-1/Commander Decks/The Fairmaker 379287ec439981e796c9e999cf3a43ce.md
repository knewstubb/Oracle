## Strategy & Playstyle

Gruul creature-based beatdown/stax deck that leverages Ruric Thar's 6-damage punisher effect to tax opponents for casting noncreature spells. The deck runs almost exclusively creatures (with creature-based ramp, removal, and card draw) to avoid triggering its own commander's ability while opponents take 6 damage for every instant, sorcery, enchantment, artifact, or planeswalker they cast.

**Primary Game Plan:** Deploy mana dorks and ramp creatures turns 1–3, slam Ruric Thar on turn 4, then ride the punisher damage while beating down with efficient Gruul creatures. Opponents face a choice: cast spells and take 6, or play suboptimally and get overrun by combat.

**Secondary Lines:** Go wide with big creature threats (Kamahl, Goreclaw, Vigor) and pump effects (Kessig Wolf Run, Champion of Lambholt) to push through damage. Collector Ouphe and Nikya of the Old Ways provide additional stax layers that lock opponents out of artifact/noncreature-based strategies.

**Win Conditions:**

- Ruric Thar damage accumulation (opponents casting 3–4 noncreature spells = 18–24 damage from Ruric alone)
- Purphoros, God of the Forge + creature spam (each creature ETB deals 2 to each opponent)
- Kamahl, Heart of Krosa + wide board (overrun-style alpha strike)
- Twinflame Tyrant + multiple attackers (stealing opponents' spells)

**Typical Game Arc:** Turns 1–3 ramp with creature-based acceleration (16 ramp sources). Turn 4–5 deploy Ruric Thar with protection (Lightning Greaves, Swiftfoot Boots). Turns 6–8 pressure opponents who can't develop their boards efficiently, close with combat damage while Ruric drains their life.

## Key Synergy Lines

### Ruric Thar Tax Engine

**Cards:** Ruric Thar + Nikya of the Old Ways + Collector Ouphe

**Steps:**

1. Ruric Thar punishes each noncreature spell with 6 damage
2. Nikya prevents YOU from casting noncreature spells (irrelevant — deck is 90%+ creatures) while doubling mana from lands
3. Collector Ouphe shuts off all activated abilities of artifacts
4. Opponents face: 6 damage per noncreature spell, no artifact utility, and you're doubling mana

**Result:** Soft lock on opponents' game plans. Combo decks, control, and spell-heavy strategies are heavily taxed. You power out creatures with doubled mana.

### Natural Order into Finisher

**Cards:** Natural Order + any green creature + (Archetype of Endurance / Vigor / Twinflame Tyrant)

**Steps:**

1. Sacrifice a mana dork or utility creature to Natural Order
2. Search your library for any green creature — deploy it for free
3. Archetype of Endurance gives hexproof to all your creatures (Ruric Thar becomes nearly untouchable)
4. Or Vigor makes combat math impossible for opponents (all damage becomes +1/+1 counters)

**Result:** Converts a low-value creature into a game-warping threat. Ruric Thar triggers once (6 to you) but the payoff is typically game-deciding.

### Yisan Creature Toolbox

**Cards:** Yisan, the Wanderer Bard + Scryb Ranger (untap Yisan) + creature suite

**Steps:**

1. Activate Yisan (verse 1) → fetch Llanowar Elves/Birds of Paradise
2. Scryb Ranger untaps Yisan (return a Forest to hand, replay it)
3. Activate Yisan again (verse 2) → fetch Sakura-Tribe Elder or Priest of Titania
4. Continue climbing the curve: verse 3 → Eternal Witness, verse 4 → Goreclaw, verse 5 → Ohran Frostfang, verse 6 → Ruric Thar

**Result:** Repeatable creature tutor that builds the board steadily. With Scryb Ranger, advance 2 verses per turn for explosive development.

### Kediss Damage Spread

**Cards:** Kediss, Emberclaw Familiar + Ruric Thar + any damage boost (Kessig Wolf Run, Shadowspear)

**Steps:**

1. Attack one opponent with Ruric Thar (6/6, must attack each combat)
2. Kediss copies combat damage dealt to each other opponent
3. Pump Ruric Thar with Kessig Wolf Run → all opponents take the pumped damage
4. With 10+ power, one attack cycle hits all three opponents for 10+

**Result:** Converts Ruric Thar's mandatory attack into a three-player clock. Eliminates the "only attacks one player" limitation of commander damage.

## Strengths & Weaknesses

### Strengths

- **Punishes spell-heavy strategies:** Control, combo, and storm decks take massive damage just trying to play their game plan — 6 per noncreature spell adds up fast in multiplayer
- **Creature-dense build is resilient to its own commander:** With 16 ramp creatures, 10 draw creatures, and 8 removal creatures, the deck rarely needs to trigger its own Ruric Thar
- **Excellent protection suite:** Lightning Greaves, Swiftfoot Boots, Heroic Intervention, Archetype of Endurance, and Saryth provide layers of defense for Ruric Thar
- **Explosive mana:** 16 ramp sources mean consistent turn 3-4 Ruric Thar deployments; Nikya doubles land mana for even faster development

### Weaknesses

- **Board wipes are painful:** The deck has no counterspells and limited recursion — a well-timed Wrath of God resets the entire mana dork army and protection pieces
- **Ruric Thar's mandatory attack:** Must attack each combat, making him vulnerable to deathtouch blockers, Maze of Ith, and combat tricks
- **Limited card advantage against resolved permanents:** If opponents land enchantment-based hate (Ghostly Prison, Propaganda) without casting spells to trigger Ruric Thar, the deck struggles to push through
- **Self-damage from necessary noncreature spells:** Natural Order, Heroic Intervention, Beast Within, and Rampant Growth all trigger 6 damage to you — must be strategic about when to cast them

## Matchup Notes

### Threats That Shut Us Down

- **Ghostly Prison / Propaganda:** Taxes combat without triggering Ruric Thar (they're already on board). → Beast Within, Bane of Progress, or Tranquil Frillback can destroy; otherwise, pressure the enchantment player politically.
- **Toxic Deluge / Cyclonic Rift:** Uncounterable board wipes that reset your entire development. → Hold Heroic Intervention or redeploy from a strong mana base. Accept the 6 self-damage for casting Heroic Intervention as insurance.
- **Maze of Ith / Labyrinth of Skophos:** Neutralizes Ruric Thar's combat each turn without casting a spell. → Kessig Wolf Run or Shadowspear trample over blockers; Bane of Progress destroys Maze.

### Favorable Matchups

- Storm and spellslinger decks — casting 5+ noncreature spells per turn means 30+ damage from Ruric Thar alone
- Blue control decks — each counterspell costs 6 life, making them choose between answers and survival
- Artifact combo decks — Collector Ouphe + Ruric Thar creates a double lock

### Table Politics

- Ruric Thar draws aggro — frame yourself as "keeping combo in check" to avoid being targeted by creature-based decks who aren't affected
- Don't deploy Ruric Thar into an open board unless you have protection; wait for haste enabler or boots in hand

## Upgrade Strategy

This deck's core tension is minimising noncreature spells while maintaining necessary interaction and ramp. Currently 8 noncreature spells remain in the list (each dealing 6 to you when cast). The primary upgrade path is replacing every sorcery/instant ramp spell with creature-based equivalents (Wood Elves over Rampant Growth, Farhaven Elf over Kodama's Reach) to eliminate self-inflicted Ruric Thar damage entirely. The secondary path improves resilience to board wipes — currently the deck's biggest weakness, since losing the mana dork army resets development by 2-3 turns. Primal Surge is the dream finisher: if the deck is 100% permanents, it puts the ENTIRE library onto the battlefield. The budget approach focuses on cheap creature-based replacements for existing sorceries, while the premium path adds Deflecting Swat (free redirect, no trigger) and Craterhoof as a Natural Order target. ## Mulligan Guide *(optional)*

**Keep hands that have:**

- 3+ lands (including green source) and at least 2 mana acceleration pieces — Ruric Thar on turn 3-4 is the goal; without fast ramp, the punisher comes online too late
- Any protection piece (Lightning Greaves, Swiftfoot Boots, Heroic Intervention) alongside ramp — Ruric Thar with boots is far stronger than a naked Ruric Thar
- A draw engine creature (Beast Whisperer, Toski, Ohran Frostfang) if the hand has adequate mana — card flow sustains the creature pressure

**Mulligan aggressively if:**

- Fewer than 3 lands and fewer than 2 mana dorks — the deck cannot function without early ramp
- All expensive finishers with no acceleration — a hand of 6-drops is unkeepable without a path to casting them quickly
