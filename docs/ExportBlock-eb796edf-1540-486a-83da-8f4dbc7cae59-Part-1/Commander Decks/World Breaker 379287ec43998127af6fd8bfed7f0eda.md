## Strategy & Playstyle

Jund land sacrifice/landfall deck that uses Hearthhull, the Worldseed to recur lands from the graveyard while generating value from landfall triggers. The deck sacrifices lands as a resource, then replays them from the graveyard to trigger landfall effects repeatedly, building a token army and overwhelming opponents through combat.

**Primary Game Plan:** Sacrifice lands to outlets (Zuran Orb, Hearthhull's own ability) to fill the graveyard, then recur them via Hearthhull, Ramunap Excavator, and other effects to trigger landfall payoffs (Omnath, Titania, Rampaging Baloths) multiple times per turn.

**Secondary Lines:** Grind value through traditional ramp into large threats. Lotus Cobra + land recursion generates burst mana for casting multiple spells. Hearthhull's 6/7 body with trample provides a credible combat clock while the engine develops.

**Win Conditions:**

- Token swarm from repeated landfall triggers (Rampaging Baloths, Titania, Omnath, Eusocial Engineering)
- Hearthhull commander damage (6/7 with trample, grows via land count)
- Zuran Orb + Ramunap Excavator + landfall payoffs (recurring land sacrifice loop for massive value turns)

**Typical Game Arc:** Turns 1–3 ramp aggressively (Sakura-Tribe Elder, Rampant Growth, Lotus Cobra). Turns 4–5 deploy Hearthhull and begin sacrificing lands. Turns 6–8 generate overwhelming board presence through recurring landfall, close out with token combat.

## Key Synergy Lines

### Zuran Orb Recursion Loop

**Cards:** Zuran Orb + Ramunap Excavator + Hearthhull

**Steps:**

1. Sacrifice a land to Zuran Orb (gain 2 life)
2. Hearthhull triggers — put a land from graveyard onto the battlefield or into hand (depending on trigger text)
3. Play the land from graveyard using Ramunap Excavator's ability
4. Each land entering triggers all landfall payoffs
5. Repeat for each available land play per turn

**Result:** Multiple landfall triggers per turn, life gain buffer, and accelerating board development. With Lotus Cobra, also generates mana for additional spells.

### Lotus Cobra Mana Engine

**Cards:** Lotus Cobra + any fetchland/land recursion + land sacrifice outlet

**Steps:**

1. Play a fetchland → Lotus Cobra triggers (add one mana of any color)
2. Sacrifice fetchland to search → land ETB triggers Cobra again
3. Net 2 mana from one land drop plus the land you searched for
4. With Ramunap Excavator, replay the fetchland from graveyard for additional triggers

**Result:** Burst mana generation that lets you chain multiple spells or deploy threats ahead of curve. Converts extra land drops into significant mana advantage.

### Titania's Land Army

**Cards:** Titania, Protector of Argoth + land sacrifice outlets (Zuran Orb, Hearthhull)

**Steps:**

1. Sacrifice a land to Zuran Orb or Hearthhull's ability
2. Titania creates a 5/3 green Elemental token
3. Recur the land from graveyard (Hearthhull, Ramunap Excavator)
4. Sacrifice again next turn for another 5/3

**Result:** Repeatable 5/3 token generation each turn cycle. Multiple sacrifices in one turn create an instant army. Tokens have real power — not chump blockers but genuine threats.

## Strengths & Weaknesses

### Strengths

- **Resilient to creature wipes:** Core value comes from lands, not creatures — Hearthhull recurs from command zone and the land-based engine rebuilds quickly after Wrath effects
- **Strong ramp package:** Lotus Cobra, Sakura-Tribe Elder, Rampant Growth, Icetill Explorer, and Spelunking provide consistent early acceleration
- **Self-sustaining engine:** Land sacrifice creates value immediately (Zuran Orb life gain, Titania tokens) while Hearthhull/Ramunap recur the sacrificed lands — the resource isn't actually spent
- **Multiple landfall payoffs:** Redundancy across Omnath, Titania, Rampaging Baloths, and Eusocial Engineering means one payoff sticking is enough

### Weaknesses

- **Blood Moon / Back to Basics:** Converts all nonbasic lands to Mountains or taps them — shuts off utility lands and color fixing simultaneously
- **Graveyard hate:** Rest in Peace and Bojuka Bog prevent land recursion, the core engine. Without graveyard access, land sacrifices become permanent losses
- **Commander-dependent:** Hearthhull is expensive (6 CMC) and the recursion loop depends on it sticking. Repeated removal taxes increase cost quickly
- **Slow without ramp:** If early ramp is disrupted, the deck is sitting on expensive cards with no way to deploy them before aggressive decks close out

## Matchup Notes

### Threats That Shut Us Down

- **Blood Moon:** Turns off Cabal Coffers, utility lands, and color fixing. → Rely on basic lands and mana dorks. Prioritize green sources in opening hands.
- **Rest in Peace / Leyline of the Void:** Prevents all land recursion from graveyard. → Shift to traditional ramp and deploy threats without relying on sacrifice loops. Hearthhull still has a 6/7 body.
- **Cyclonic Rift:** Bounces all tokens and enchantments, resetting multiple turns of landfall work. → Hold up key pieces and redeploy post-Rift; land base remains intact.

### Favorable Matchups

- Slow control decks — we generate value faster than they can answer it; lands are hard to interact with
- Creature combat decks — Titania's 5/3 tokens and wide boards from landfall outscale most creature strategies
- Spell-heavy decks with few creatures — our tokens don't rely on creature density, and edict effects from the precon shell can still pressure

### Table Politics

- The land-based engine looks innocent ("just playing lands") until Titania drops and suddenly you have 20 power on board. Use this perception gap.
- Avoid deploying all payoffs simultaneously — stagger them to reduce the chance of a single board wipe being devastating

## Upgrade Strategy

This deck already runs all the highest-synergy pieces from EDHREC (Gitrog Monster, Splendid Reclamation, Aftermath Analyst, Oracle of Mul Daya, Titania, Korvold, Omnath). The next upgrade tier focuses on two paths: (1) adding explosive one-shot finishers like Scapeshift that convert a developed board into an instant win by sacrificing all lands and recurring them, or (2) adding card selection and resilience pieces like Valakut Exploration and Crucible of Worlds that keep the engine online through disruption. The precon mod constraints (10 swaps max) limit immediate changes, but once the pod agrees to uncap modifications, Scapeshift + Splendid Reclamation back-to-back is the deck's most devastating play pattern. ## Mulligan Guide *(optional)*

**Keep hands that have:**

- 3+ lands including at least one green source, plus a 2-drop ramp spell (Rampant Growth, Sakura-Tribe Elder, Lotus Cobra) — Hearthhull costs 6, so early ramp is non-negotiable
- Any landfall payoff (Omnath, Titania, Rampaging Baloths) in combination with 4+ lands — late-game value hands are keepable if you can survive to deploy them
- A land recursion piece (Ramunap Excavator) plus a sacrifice outlet — the engine can bootstrap from just these two pieces

**Mulligan aggressively if:**

- Fewer than 3 lands with no ramp spells — the deck cannot function without hitting land drops every turn for the first 5 turns
