# Yedora the Explorer

Bracket: 3
Card Count: 102
Colours: G
Commander: Yedora, Grave Gardener
Deck URL: https://archidekt.com/decks/21109505
Last Updated: June 9, 2026

## Strategy & Playstyle

Mono-green landfall/recursion combo deck. Uses Yedora's death trigger to return creatures as Forests, then bounces and recasts them for infinite landfall and death triggers. Wins through overwhelming token armies or infinite mana combos.

**Primary Game Plan:** Assemble Yedora + a sacrifice outlet + a morph/face-down creature to create a repeatable loop — each creature dies, returns as a Forest via Yedora's trigger, gets bounced to hand (Quirion Ranger, Scryb Ranger, Stampeding Wildebeests), and recast face-down for infinite landfall, death triggers, and mana.

**Secondary Lines:** Go wide with repeated landfall payoffs — Scute Swarm, Rampaging Baloths, and Avenger of Zendikar generate an army off the core loop's landfall triggers even without achieving a true infinite.

**Win Conditions:**

- Yedora + Ashnod's Altar + Den Protector/Ainok Survivalist/Nantuko Vigilante (infinite colorless mana + infinite death triggers — win with any payoff)
- Life and Limb + Sporemound (NOT currently in deck) — infinite Saprolands (each is a Forest and a Saproling, triggering Sporemound indefinitely)
- Proteus Machine + Yedora (two-card infinite — Machine sacrifices itself, returns as Forest, tap Forest for mana, recast Machine, repeat)
- Yedora + Embodiment of Insight + Zuran Orb (infinite life + infinite landfall — Forests become creatures, die to Zuran Orb, return via Yedora)

**Typical Game Arc:** Turns 1–3 deploy ramp and mana dorks. Turn 4–5 land Yedora. Turn 5–7 begin assembling loops — the deck threatens a combo finish from turn 6 onward but generates incremental value even while setting up.

## Key Synergy Lines

### Ashnod's Altar + Morph Creature Loop

**Cards:** Yedora, Grave Gardener + Ashnod's Altar + Den Protector (or Ainok Survivalist, Nantuko Vigilante, Hauntwoods Shrieker, Nervous Gardener)

**Steps:**

1. With Yedora and Ashnod's Altar in play, sacrifice a morph creature (e.g., Den Protector face-down) to Altar for {2}
2. Yedora's trigger returns the creature face-down as a Forest
3. Tap the new Forest for {G}, then use {2} from Altar + {G} to cast the creature face-down again for {3}
4. Repeat — net infinite landfall triggers, infinite death triggers, infinite colorless mana

**Result:** Infinite mana, infinite death/landfall triggers. Den Protector variant also returns all cards from graveyard to hand. Ainok Survivalist/Nantuko Vigilante destroy all opponents' artifacts and enchantments.

### Proteus Machine Self-Loop

**Cards:** Yedora, Grave Gardener + Proteus Machine

**Steps:**

1. Proteus Machine is face-down (a 2/2 creature)
2. It dies — Yedora returns it face-down as a Forest
3. Tap the Forest for {G}. Accumulate {3} over cycles (or with other mana)
4. Actually: Proteus Machine's morph ability lets it flip to become an artifact creature — once it's a creature again, sacrifice to any outlet and repeat

**Result:** With any free sacrifice outlet, this is a two-card infinite with Yedora. Generates infinite green mana, death triggers, and landfall triggers.

### Temur Charger + Evolutionary Leap Engine

**Cards:** Yedora, Grave Gardener + Temur Charger + Evolutionary Leap

**Steps:**

1. Sacrifice Temur Charger to Evolutionary Leap (pay {G}, reveal cards until a creature, put it in hand)
2. Yedora returns Temur Charger face-down as a Forest
3. Temur Charger's morph trigger: when a permanent is turned face-up, return Temur Charger to hand. Since it entered as a Forest (face-down permanent), the next face-up trigger returns it
4. Recast Temur Charger face-down for {3}, sacrifice again — repeat

**Result:** Put all creature cards from library into hand. Infinite death triggers and landfall triggers.

### Embodiment of Insight + Zuran Orb Loop

**Cards:** Yedora, Grave Gardener + Embodiment of Insight + Zuran Orb

**Steps:**

1. Embodiment of Insight animates Forests as 3/3 creatures via landfall
2. Sacrifice an animated Forest to Zuran Orb (gain 2 life)
3. The animated Forest was a creature — Yedora returns it face-down as a new Forest
4. New Forest entering triggers Embodiment of Insight's landfall — animate another Forest
5. Repeat with the new animated Forest

**Result:** Infinite life, infinite landfall triggers, infinite death triggers. Win with any landfall payoff (Scute Swarm, Rampaging Baloths).

### Value Engine: Bounce + Landfall Payoffs

**Cards:** Yedora + Quirion Ranger/Scryb Ranger + Landfall payoff (Scute Swarm, Rampaging Baloths, Avenger of Zendikar)

**Steps:**

1. Any creature dies with Yedora out — returns as a Forest
2. Quirion Ranger or Scryb Ranger bounces the Forest back to hand (as their untap ability)
3. Replay the Forest — triggers all landfall payoffs
4. Not truly infinite (limited by creatures dying) but generates enormous value over a game

**Result:** Repeated landfall triggers generating tokens (Scute Swarm copies, 4/4 Beasts, Plant tokens). Builds an overwhelming board without needing a true combo.

## Strengths & Weaknesses

### Strengths

- **Multiple redundant combo lines** — Ashnod's Altar combos with 5+ different morph creatures in the deck, making it very hard to shut down all angles simultaneously
- **Resilient recursion engine** — Yedora makes creatures nearly impossible to permanently remove; every kill spell your opponents cast feeds your engine
- **Generates value without comboing** — even without assembling an infinite loop, the deck produces repeated landfall triggers, card draw (Tireless Tracker, Beast Whisperer), and token armies
- **Low-cost combo pieces** — most combo components are cheap morphs ({3} to cast face-down), meaning the deck can rebuild quickly after disruption
- **Commander is affordable to recast** — at 5 CMC with heavy ramp (16 ramp sources), Yedora comes back quickly after removal

### Weaknesses

- **Graveyard hate is devastating** — Rest in Peace, Leyline of the Void, and Dauthi Voidwalker prevent Yedora's trigger from resolving entirely. The deck has no way to remove enchantments at instant speed besides Beast Within
- **No counterspells or stack interaction** — mono-green cannot protect combo turns from removal; opponents can respond to the sacrifice trigger with exile effects (Swords to Plowshares)
- **Telegraphed strategy** — Yedora + sacrifice outlet on board signals "combo next turn," giving opponents a full turn cycle to find answers
- **Vulnerable to artifact/enchantment hate on your pieces** — Life and Limb, Ashnod's Altar, and Evolutionary Leap are all critical but fragile permanents
- **Card count at 115** — the deck is currently 15 cards over the legal limit of 100, meaning significant cuts are needed before it can be played

## Matchup Notes

### Threats That Shut Us Down

- **Rest in Peace / Leyline of the Void:** Completely disables Yedora's trigger — creatures go to exile, never return as Forests → Response: Cankerbloom, Ainok Survivalist, Nantuko Vigilante can destroy enchantments, but only at sorcery speed. Prioritize holding removal for these
- **Grafdigger's Cage:** Prevents creatures from entering the battlefield from graveyards → Response: Beast Within, Cankerbloom. Note: technically Yedora returns cards "to the battlefield face down" which may not be blocked by Cage (they return as lands, not creatures from graveyard)
- **Exile-based removal (Swords to Plowshares, Path to Exile):** Bypasses Yedora entirely since the creature never hits the graveyard → Response: Lightning Greaves, Swiftfoot Boots, Heroic Intervention, Tamiyo's Safekeeping. Deploy protection before combo turns
- **Stax that prevents land ETB (Confounding Conundrum):** Punishes the core landfall loop → Response: Beast Within is the primary answer; play through it by not bouncing lands

### Favorable Matchups

- **Creature-based aggro/voltron:** Every creature they kill with combat feeds Yedora's engine — attacks into our board actually help us
- **Attrition/grindy midrange:** We out-grind anyone because our creatures never truly die — we convert opponent removal into our own resources
- **Decks slow to interact:** If opponents spend turns 1–5 developing without holding removal, we assemble loops before they can respond

### Table Politics

- **Fly under the radar early** — ramp and morph creatures look unthreatening. Don't deploy Yedora until you have protection or can immediately start a loop
- **Leverage "I'm just making Forests" perception** — the deck looks like mono-green ramp until the combo turn. Let aggressive players attract removal while you set up

## Upgrade Strategy

This deck's primary weakness is that it generates infinite loops but lacks reliable win conditions to close the game once the loop is active. The main upgrade path is adding dedicated kill shots — Altar of Dementia and Blasting Station both convert infinite death triggers into a win on the spot, eliminating the current reliance on combat damage through tokens. A secondary path adds resilience against graveyard hate (the deck's Achilles heel) by adding redundancy pieces like Phyrexian Altar that enable loops without relying on Yedora's trigger resolving. The budget-conscious approach focuses on Altar of Dementia (owned, free) and cheap morph utility creatures, while the no-budget path adds Phyrexian Altar as an upgrade over Ashnod's Altar (produces coloured mana, enabling face-down recasts without additional sources). ## Mulligan Guide *(optional)*

**Keep hands that have:**

- 3+ lands (including at least one Forest) and 1–2 ramp spells — Yedora costs 5, so you need to cast her by turn 3–4
- A morph creature or bounce effect in addition to lands/ramp — this means you can immediately start looping once Yedora lands
- Protection piece (Greaves, Boots, Heroic Intervention) alongside ramp — Yedora is the engine, protecting her is critical

**Mulligan aggressively if:**

- Fewer than 3 lands — even with mana dorks, a 2-land hand is too risky for a 5-CMC commander
- All payoffs, no engine — a hand of Avenger of Zendikar + Scute Swarm + Rampaging Baloths with no ramp or morph creatures does nothing for several turns
- No green sources — the deck is mono-green but Guildless Commons and Rogue's Passage can't cast your spells alone
